TiTok: An Image is Worth 32 Tokens for Reconstruction and Generation (arXiv:2406.07550)

We propose TiTok, a compact 1D tokenizer leveraging region redundancy to represent an image with only 32 tokens for image reconstruction and generation. Conventional image tokenizers encode images as 2D grids of tokens, which suffers from high redundancy and inefficiency. TiTok breaks the conventional 2D grid constraint by treating image patches as a 1D sequence and learning compact latent codes. Using VQ-VAE based approach, TiTok achieves state-of-the-art generation performance on ImageNet while being substantially faster in both training and inference. Our experiments demonstrate that 32 tokens can faithfully reconstruct images at 256x256 resolution, revealing significant spatial redundancy in conventional 2D tokenization. TiTok enables efficient autoregressive image generation with dramatically reduced sequence length, making it practical for large-scale training.

Keywords: image tokenizer, 1D token, VQ-VAE, discrete representation, codebook, image generation, image reconstruction, autoregressive, token efficiency, spatial redundancy, compact representation, visual tokenization
